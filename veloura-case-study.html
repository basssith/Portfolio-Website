Case Study

Veloura Perfume App

Bridging the gap between digital interfaces and physical senses through immersive design.

Role UI/UX & Branding
Timeline 4 Weeks
Tools Figma, After Effects
Platform iOS Mobile App
01 / Overview

The Sensory Challenge

Purchasing perfumes online is historically a blind guess. Unlike clothing or electronics, scent is entirely physical. Customers are often hesitant to invest in luxury fragrances online because they cannot perceive the product’s true character, leading to low conversions and high return rates.

The goal of Veloura was to design an e-commerce platform that evokes a sensory response through storytelling, visual notes exploration, and a personalized recommendation engine.

The Solution

  • Scent-Matching Algorithm: A guided onboarding quiz translating mood, ingredients, and lifestyle into personalized fragrance suggestions.
  • Interactive Notes Pyramid: A visual mapping of Top, Heart, and Base notes so users understand how a perfume unfolds over time.
  • Mood-Driven Art Direction: High-end lifestyle imagery and descriptive words that trigger olfactory imagination (e.g., "crisp morning rain", "warm cedarwood").
Stakeholders & Constraints

Project Alignment

Product Owners

Goal: Drive online conversions by 25% and minimize high return rates of luxury fragrance bottles by ensuring users select the right scent profile.

Fragrance Experts

Goal: Authentically map the complex scent structure (top, heart, base notes) and categorize scent families to prevent misleading descriptions.

Development Team

Constraint: Maintain a lightweight, optimized codebase since the app utilizes layered, micro-interaction rich screens.

Target User Persona

02 / UX Research

Understanding User Behavior

We conducted qualitative interviews and survey analysis with fragrance buyers. The research yielded three crucial insights:

  • Visual Trust: Users buy fragrances based on ingredients (citrus, wood, floral) and the color palette associated with the product page.
  • The Scent Evolution: Many buyers don’t realize that perfumes change scent profiles over the hours. They feel cheated when the initial smell morphs.
  • Confidence Deficit: 67% of interviewees expressed low confidence in purchasing an unsmelled bottle over $100.
Our Approach

The Design Process

Following a double-diamond design methodology to explore user needs deeply before crafting high-fidelity interfaces.

01

Empathize

Conducted 12 qualitative interviews with fragrance buyers to identify purchasing patterns, friction points, and confidence criteria.

02

Define

Mapped user pain points and created target personas. Defined core focus: translating smell into visual and lifestyle associations.

03

Ideate

Sketched layouts for scent finder quiz flows and designed the multi-layered interactive Notes Pyramid framework.

04

Prototype

Built micro-interactions in Figma and After Effects to demonstrate smooth transitioning notes and quiz card card-swipes.

05

Test

Ran usability tests with 8 participants. Iterated on typography hierarchy and simplified notes descriptions for clearer comprehension.

03 / Core Features

Visualizing Scent Electronically

1. Scent Discovery Quiz

An interactive, highly-visual onboarding quiz mapping user fragrance preferences (e.g. fresh morning air vs. smoky lounge) to recommend 3 signature bottles.

2. Interactive Notes Pyramid

An expanding pyramid interface charting the Top Notes (lasts 15m), Heart Notes (lasts 4h), and Base Notes (lasts 24h) to manage expectations of scent morphing.

3. Sensory Art Direction

Using custom ambient backgrounds, color grading that reflects scent notes, and clean minimalist typography to evoke a feeling of premium luxury.

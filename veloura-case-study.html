Challenges

The primary challenge focused on translating complex, sensory fragrance details into a clear and accessible mobile interface. The design needed to address the inherent friction of buying fragrances online without physically smelling them. Presenting detailed ingredient structures (top, heart, and base notes) in a way that feels simple, visual, and non-academic was key to keeping the shopping flow intuitive.

Solutions

The solution centered around sensory art direction and clean structure. We introduced an onboarding Scent Discovery Quiz to map user mood profiles and ingredients directly to personalized matches, reducing shopping anxiety. Additionally, we created an interactive visual Notes Pyramid displaying how a scent evolves over the day, fostering transparency and consumer confidence.

Stakeholders Alignment

Ensuring that the digital product aligns with different strategic business goals was vital. We mapped requirements from three main stakeholder perspectives to balance aesthetic, conversion, and performance factors.

Product Owners

Goal: Lift digital conversion rates by 25% and reduce customer returns by offering high-fidelity visual matching features.

Fragrance Houses / Experts

Goal: Guarantee ingredient authenticity and structural mapping (scent pyramid notes) to respect fragrance heritage.

Developers

Constraint: Standardize clean components and design tokens to ensure fluid 60fps micro-animations on mobile displays.

The Design Process

We applied a methodical, user-centered approach to investigate friction, ideate visually, prototype tactile interactions, and test with real users.

01

Empathize

Conducted 12 qualitative interviews with fragrance buyers to identify purchasing patterns, friction points, and confidence criteria.

02

Define

Mapped user pain points and created target personas. Defined core focus: translating smell into visual and lifestyle associations.

03

Ideate

Sketched layouts for scent finder quiz flows and designed the multi-layered interactive Notes Pyramid framework.

04

Prototype

Built micro-interactions in Figma and After Effects to demonstrate smooth transitioning notes and quiz card card-swipes.

05

Test

Ran usability tests with 8 participants. Iterated on typography hierarchy and simplified notes descriptions for clearer comprehension.
